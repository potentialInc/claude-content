# Brand Voice

## Purpose
Define and document your brand's unique voice and tone to ensure consistent, authentic communication across all channels and team members.

## When to Use
- Creating brand voice guidelines
- Onboarding new content creators
- Ensuring consistency across channels
- Defining brand personality
- Training team on brand communication
- Auditing existing content for consistency

## Voice vs Tone

**Voice**: Your brand's personality‚Äîconsistent across all content
**Tone**: How voice adapts to different situations‚Äîvaries by context

**Analogy**:
- **Voice** = Your personality (always you)
- **Tone** = Your mood/expression (changes based on situation)

**Example**:
- **Voice**: Friendly, helpful, knowledgeable
- **Tone** (Welcome email): Warm, excited
- **Tone** (Error message): Apologetic, helpful
- **Tone** (Technical docs): Professional, clear

### Step 4: Write Do's and Don'ts

**Specific writing guidance**:

**Example**:
```
‚úÖ DO:
- Use contractions (we're, don't, it's)
- Write like you talk
- Use "you" and "we"

‚ùå DON'T:
- Use corporate jargon ("leverage synergies")
```

## Voice Dimensions

### Formality Spectrum

**Formal ‚Üí Casual**

**Formal**:
> "We appreciate your business and look forward to serving your needs."

**Balanced**:
> "Thanks for choosing us. We're here to help!"

**Casual**:
> "Hey! Glad you're here. Let's get started."

**Your brand position**: [Define where you fall]

### Respectful ‚Üí Irreverent

**Respectful**:
> "We understand this is frustrating. Here's how we can help."

**Balanced**:
> "This is annoying, we know. Let's fix it."

**Irreverent**:
> "Well, that's not supposed to happen. Oops."

**Enthusiastic**:
> "We're so excited to announce our new feature! üéâ"

## Tone Variations by Context

**Define how tone shifts while voice stays consistent**:

### Success/Celebration

**When**: User accomplishes something, positive news

**Tone**: Enthusiastic, congratulatory

**Example**:
> "Woohoo! You just published your first blog post. That's awesome!"

### Error/Problem

**When**: Something goes wrong, user frustrated

**Tone**: Apologetic, helpful, clear

**Example**:
> "Oops, something went wrong. Here's how to fix it: [clear steps]"

### Onboarding/Education

**When**: Teaching new users, explaining features

**Tone**: Patient, encouraging, clear

**Example**:
> "Let's start with the basics. First, you'll create your profile. Take your time‚Äîwe'll walk you through each step."

### Marketing/Sales

**When**: Promoting product, trying to convert

**Tone**: Enthusiastic but not pushy, benefit-focused

**Example**:
> "Ready to save 10 hours a week? Our automation tools do the repetitive work so you can focus on what matters."

### Technical/Documentation

**When**: API docs, troubleshooting, technical guides

**Tone**: Clear, precise, helpful

**Example**:
> "To authenticate, include your API key in the Authorization header: `Bearer YOUR_API_KEY`"

### Serious/Sensitive Topics

**When**: Security issues, pricing changes, service disruptions

**Tone**: Serious, transparent, respectful

**Example**:
> "We're changing our pricing on March 1st. Here's exactly what's changing and why."

- ‚úÖ Use sparingly for genuine excitement

- ‚úÖ Use for parenthetical thoughts‚Äîlike this‚Äîwhen commas feel too weak

## Brand Voice Dos and Don'ts Quick Reference

### DO:

**‚úÖ Be human**
> "We messed up. Here's what happened and how we're fixing it."

**‚úÖ Use simple language**
> "Our tool helps you write faster" (not "Our solution optimizes content creation workflows")

**‚úÖ Be specific**
> "Save 5 hours per week" (not "Save significant time")

**‚úÖ Show personality**
> Include appropriate humor, warmth, or quirkiness

**‚úÖ Address the reader directly**
> "You can..." not "Users can..."

**‚úÖ Be conversational**
> Write like you talk

**‚úÖ Be concise**
> Use short sentences. Cut unnecessary words.

### DON'T:

**‚ùå Use jargon**
> Avoid "leverage synergies to maximize ROI"

**‚ùå Be vague**
> Not "We're pretty good" but "We're ranked #1 by G2"

**‚ùå Talk down to readers**
> No condescending tone

**‚ùå Be overly salesy**
> Not "BUY NOW! LIMITED TIME!"

**‚ùå Use passive voice**
> "The feature was launched" ‚Üí "We launched the feature"

**‚ùå Be inconsistent**
> Don't switch between formal and casual randomly

**‚ùå Forget the context**
> Jokes in error messages might not land

## Maintaining Consistency

### Onboarding New Writers

**Include in onboarding**:
- Voice guide review (mandatory reading)
- Real examples from your content
- Practice exercises
- Review process with feedback

### Regular Audits

**Quarterly voice audit**:
- Review recent content across channels
- Identify inconsistencies
- Update guidelines if needed
- Share examples (good and bad) with team

### Voice Champion

**Designate someone to**:
- Answer voice questions
- Review content for consistency
- Update the voice guide
- Train new team members

## Testing Your Voice

### Before Finalizing

**Ask these questions**:
1. Does it sound like us?
2. Is it distinct from competitors?
3. Does it resonate with our audience?
4. Can team members apply it consistently?
5. Does it work across all channels?

### Voice Consistency Check

**For any piece of content, ask**:
- [ ] Uses our preferred vocabulary?
- [ ] Matches our formality level?
- [ ] Tone appropriate for context?
- [ ] Sounds human, not corporate?
- [ ] Aligns with brand personality?

### 2. Too Vague
‚ùå **Don't**: "We're professional and friendly"
‚úÖ **Do**: "We're friendly‚Äîlike a knowledgeable coworker, not a best friend. We use contractions and humor but never slang."

### 3. No Examples
‚ùå **Don't**: Just list attributes
‚úÖ **Do**: Show real before/after examples

### 4. Set It and Forget It
‚ùå **Don't**: Create guide and never update
